Cheese Client 3.1: Compatible with Both Fabric & Forge

We are thrilled to announce Cheese Client 3.1, a groundbreaking update that unifies the Fabric and Forge ecosystems for Minecraft 1.12.2. For the first time, you can seamlessly run mods from both loaders alongside Cheese Client's full feature set — without compromise on performance or stealth. This release represents a major engineering milestone and opens up endless possibilities for modded gameplay.

🧩 Bridging Two Worlds: How We Made It Possible

Fabric and Forge use fundamentally different approaches to mod loading and bytecode manipulation. To make them coexist peacefully, we developed a custom hybrid classloader that intercepts and reconciles conflicting transformations. The loader delegates Fabric's Mixin patches and Forge's CoreMods to a unified pipeline, ensuring that both sets of modifications are applied in the correct order and without overlap. This process is completely transparent — you just drop your mods into the respective mods and fabric-mods folders, and Cheese Client handles the rest.

Solving the Mixin/CoreMod Conflict

The most challenging aspect was managing the interaction between Fabric's Mixin and Forge's runtime deobfuscation. Our new bytecode mediation layer analyzes every transformation in real time, detects potential conflicts (like two mods trying to patch the same method), and automatically applies a safe merge strategy. In rare cases where manual intervention is needed, the client logs a clear warning and suggests a load order. Internal tests on 50+ mod combinations showed zero crashes due to loader conflicts.

⚡ Performance: No Sacrifices

Combining two mod loaders often introduces overhead, but Cheese Client 3.1's optimized architecture keeps performance at its peak. We've implemented lazy class loading and shared caches for deobfuscation mappings, reducing memory usage by 30% compared to running Fabric and Forge separately. Additionally, our rendering engine (upgraded in 3.0) now batch-processes entity models from both ecosystems, so you won't see FPS drops even with dozens of modded blocks and entities on screen.

🧪 Stress test results: We loaded 30 Fabric mods (like Energized Power and Unwrecked Ships) and 20 Forge mods (like Thermal Dynamics and custom NPCs) simultaneously on a low-end i5 machine. Cheese Client 3.1 maintained a stable 75 FPS, while a standard Forge+Fabric setup using a hacky compatibility layer dropped to 20 FPS and frequently crashed. One beta tester remarked: “I can finally use my favorite Fabric optimisation mods alongside the Forge content mods I love — and it actually runs better than vanilla Forge!”

🎯 Enhanced Features, Same Undetectability

All Cheese Client modules (KillAura, Scaffold, Velocity, etc.) remain fully compatible with this hybrid environment. We've updated the anti‑detection systems to account for the modified packet flows that mods can introduce. The client now dynamically adjusts its timing and randomization based on the current mod load, ensuring that you stay under the radar even on servers that check for modded clients. The new “Mod-Aware” profile in the settings panel automatically applies the safest bypasses when mods are detected.

Easy Setup & Mod Management

Getting started is simple: install Cheese Client 3.1, then drop your Fabric mods (JAR files) into the fabric-mods folder and your Forge mods into the mods folder, just as you normally would. The client includes a built-in mod compatibility checker that highlights any known conflicts and offers to disable problematic mods. You can also create separate profiles for different mod combinations and switch between them with one click — perfect for players who join both vanilla and heavily modded servers.

🔮 What's Next for 1.12.2 and Beyond

Version 3.1 is focused on 1.12.2 because it remains the most popular version for modded Minecraft. However, the technology we've developed is version-agnostic. We are already experimenting with backports to 1.8.9 and forward ports to 1.16.5, and we plan to extend Fabric/Forge compatibility to those versions later this year. Additionally, we're working with popular mod authors to create “Cheese Edition” mods that take advantage of our unified API to offer features not possible in either loader alone.

Community-Driven Development

This release wouldn't have been possible without feedback from our community. The idea for unified mod support came from a thread on our Discord, and over 100 users participated in the private beta. We invite you to join the discussion and help shape the future of Cheese Client — whether you're a modpack developer, a PvP player, or just someone who wants the best of both worlds.

Cheese Client 3.1 is available now via the built-in updater or from our download page. Existing 3.0 users will be prompted to update on next launch. As always, this update is free for all licensed users. Dive into the ultimate modded Minecraft experience today!
