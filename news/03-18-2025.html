March 18, 2025 Development Team TECHNICAL

Advanced Movement Module:
Parkour & Speed Enhancements

We are thrilled to unveil the latest technical evolution in Cheese Client: a completely redesigned movement module that sets a new standard for agility and stealth. This update focuses on three core pillars—parkour assistance, intelligent speed control, and natural motion simulation—to give you an unparalleled edge in both PvP and parkour scenarios.

🧀 Smarter Parkour Assistance

Our new parkour system goes beyond simple auto‑jump. It now evaluates block geometry, edge distances, and landing momentum in real time. The module can chain jumps across tricky gaps, automatically sprint-jump on optimal ticks, and even handle multi-level ascents like spiral staircases with zero input. During internal tests on competitive parkour servers, users cleared courses 15–20% faster while using 30% fewer actions — making movement flow much more naturally.

Precision edge detection

By scanning blocks up to 4 blocks ahead, the movement assistant predicts the exact take‑off point for maximum jump efficiency. It adapts to slabs, trapdoors, and even soul sand, reducing the risk of “sticky” landings. All this runs within the new predictive trajectory engine introduced in v3.1 core.

⚡ Adaptive Speed Control

Speed enhancements are no longer a blunt “toggle max velocity”. We developed an inertia‑aware speed governor that dynamically scales your velocity based on server TPS, surrounding entities, and turning radius. In open corridors you'll feel a responsive boost; in tight spaces or crowded servers the module eases off to avoid collision flags. The result is a speed that feels both powerful and human-like, drastically reducing anti‑cheat violations.

✨ Did you know? The new speed algorithm uses a feedback loop similar to cruise control. It continuously adjusts to keep you within 98–102% of the vanilla max speed while making micro‑adjustments that mimic mouse‑turning inconsistencies. Perfect for servers running NoCheatPlus or AAC.

🎯 Natural Movement Patterns & Smoothing

Perhaps the most sophisticated addition is the humanization layer. The module interpolates your input to remove robotic snapping — strafes become rounded, jump arcs gain slight variation, and landing impacts are cushioned with subtle “pre‑movement”. This isn't just visual; it alters packet timing to replicate the tiny delays of a real player. Combined with the new buffered sprint system, you'll be nearly indistinguishable from a high‑skill vanilla user.

Configurable profiles for every playstyle

All features are adjustable via the Cheese Client settings panel (or the in‑game HUD). You can dial in the level of assistance: from a light nudge for legit players to an advanced “flow” mode for speedrunners. And like every Cheese module, it supports per‑server profiles so you never have to re‑tweak after switching gamemodes.
